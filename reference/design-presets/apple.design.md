# Apple Inspired DESIGN.md

Source note:
- Inspired by the public Apple entry from VoltAgent `awesome-design-md` and `getdesign.md/apple/design-md`.
- Public summary used as seed: premium white space, SF Pro, cinematic imagery.
- This is a local Basalt preset for prompt steering, not an official Apple design system.

## 1. Visual Theme & Atmosphere
- Use a premium, quiet, high-confidence aesthetic.
- Prefer bright surfaces, restrained contrast, and large open space over dense layouts.
- The page should feel polished and expensive without looking ornamental.
- Favor calm composition, precise alignment, and deliberate pacing between sections.

## 2. Color Palette & Roles
- Background: near-white or very light neutral.
- Foreground: near-black or deep graphite.
- Secondary text: muted neutral gray with strong readability.
- Accent usage should be rare. Prefer monochrome foundations with one restrained accent only if the product already uses one.
- Avoid loud gradients, neon glows, saturated brand blocks, and playful rainbow palettes.

## 3. Typography Rules
- Prefer SF Pro when already available in the target project. Otherwise use a clean modern sans fallback.
- Headings should be crisp and confident, usually medium to semibold rather than overly heavy.
- Body copy should be compact, highly legible, and visually quiet.
- Use larger headline scale only for hero moments; keep the rest of the hierarchy controlled and minimal.
- Tighten copy length. Apple-like pages feel edited, not verbose.

## 4. Component Stylings
- Buttons should feel solid and minimal: subtle radius, strong contrast, no exaggerated shadows.
- Cards and panels should be sparse, with careful padding and almost invisible borders or soft elevation.
- Inputs should be clean and quiet, with clear focus states and no noisy outlines.
- Navigation should feel thin, disciplined, and understated.
- Iconography should be simple and precise, never decorative for its own sake.

## 5. Layout Principles
- White space is a primary design tool. Increase breathing room before adding decoration.
- Use a simple vertical rhythm with generous section spacing.
- Containers should feel centered and stable, with wide margins on desktop and clean compression on mobile.
- Prefer a few strong sections over many small blocks.
- Cinematic visual areas are acceptable, but they should stay uncluttered and text-light.

## 6. Depth & Elevation
- Use subtle depth only where it improves hierarchy.
- Prefer soft shadows, light borders, gentle blur, and tonal separation.
- Avoid stacked shadow systems, glassmorphism overload, or heavy layered effects.

## 7. Motion
- Motion should be sparse and refined.
- Prefer fade, slight translate, and measured timing.
- Avoid playful bounce, elastic motion, or attention-grabbing looping effects.

## 8. Do
- Build layouts that feel premium through restraint.
- Keep copy short and highly curated.
- Use imagery or placeholders in a cinematic, edge-to-edge, or carefully framed way when the task calls for visuals.
- Keep surfaces clean and let spacing carry the composition.

## 9. Don't
- Do not produce startup-style gradient overload.
- Do not create noisy dashboards unless the task explicitly asks for one.
- Do not use oversized pills, excessive badges, or decorative chrome.
- Do not mix too many accent colors or competing card treatments.
